Hibiscus Tequila Sparkler

Tart-sweet and shockingly pink hibiscus syrup is the star of this bright and refreshing cocktail.

This recipe makes enough syrup for 16 drinks (great for a crowd), but if you have any leftover, there are endless creative ways to use it up. Drizzle it over icecream, use as a sweetener for lemonade or iced tea, mix into gin or tequila based cocktails, or even whisk into salad dressings.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup dried hibiscus flowers
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 oz tequila blanco
  • 1/2 oz. fresh lime juice
  • 3 sprigs mint
  • 2 oz dry, sprakling rose
  • Raspberry sorbet (for serving, optional)

    • Steps

    1. Combine hibiscus and 1 cup boiling water in a heatproof measuring cup or small bowl; let sit 10 minutes. Strain tea through a fine-mesh sieve into another measuring glass or small bowl; discard hibiscus. Add sugar to tea and stir to dissolve. Let hibiscus syrup cool.
    2. To make 1 cocktail, combine tequila, lime juice, 1/2 oz. hibiscus syrup, and 3 mint leaves in a cocktail shaker. Fill shaker with ice, cover, and shake until outside of shaker is frosty, about 20 seconds. Strain into a Champagne flute, and gradually add rose stopping 1/2" from top of glass. Top cocktail with a small scoop of raspberry sorbet if using and garnish with mint sprig.

      3. Do ahead: Hibiscus syrup can be made 3 weeks ahead. TGransfer to an airtight container; cover and chill.
